Problem definition

Poaching in Africa is a serious problem: i.e. in 10 years time (2004-2014) the forest elephant population declined by

78-81% -- more than 25,000 elephants

(Poulsen et al., 2017)

If no policies will be introduced, the rhino population in Africa will become

extinct in 2035

(Barichievy et al., 2017)
Lack of data regarding

poachers and their locations

Goal for the project

Provide definition for policy explicity -
  • Gain insight in

    potential poaching behaviour

  • Create a platform that uses RL to create

    new policies

    , train them and visualize their effects
  • Define a set of

    pre-made policies

    and their results

    after training

  • Customize

    existing RL algorithms

    to our specific case
Provide tools to do research to build towards

stopping poaching!

Approximate Q-learning

Introduce heuristics to help the model

  • An animal is within two cells distance ➟ poach the animal
  • Etc.
The agent converges on this 10x10 grid and even shows some interesting behaviour!
Why did we not stick to this system?
  • We want both agents to learn!
  • Not enough stochasticity in the way animals are poached
  • No way to add additional noise / information to the system

Research question 2

What are the quality requirements for

generated sensor-data

?

Based on the policies we tested:


Tourist noise

greatly distracts the ranger agent, often rendering it useless

Filter blur

sends the ranger in the good direction, but performs worse than exact signal

Detection chance variations

influence the variance of the ranger agent (following a single path)

Interesting poaching behavior

Not always interesting ranger behaviour

Noise is a large contribution to the behaviour of the ranger
